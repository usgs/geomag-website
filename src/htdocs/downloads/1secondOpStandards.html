1-Second Operational Standards for Intermagnet

The purpose of this letter is to help develop consensus on operational standards that are realistically needed for 1-second ground-based magnetometer systems. Several academic and governmental agencies are involved with the collection of 1-second data from variometer and observatory stations, and others are developing 1-second acquisition systems. Ideally, these data should be of sufficient quality to meet the needs of the scientific community, regardless of the particular system used to collect the data and regardless of the agency or program responsible for its collection.

As many of you probably know, Intermagnet has traditionally been involved with the acquisition of 1-minute average data, for which it has a clearly defined set of operational standards. Now, Intermagnet needs to establish standards for 1-second acquisition. Therefore, I am seeking your feedback as to the standards that must be met for the user community. It is important to recognize that this might be, and generally will be, distinct from what is actually possible from an operational standpoint. So, for example, it might be that from a user's perspective, a time-stamp on each individual 1-second datum should have a particular accuracy, say 0.01 seconds. But operationally, perhaps using GPS systems, it might be possible to obtain a time stamp accurate to better than even 0.000001 seconds. The standard, then, would be set at 0.01 seconds, since that is what is needed, not what is possible. The objective here being that established standards should be based upon strict scientific necessity, avoiding the excessively stringent and unrealistically lofty.

Personally, I already have some opinions on these matters. But the purpose of this letter is to obtain some objective response from the community. Therefore, let me pose questions on six separate issues:

Questionnaire:

  1. Data Resolution: What standard should be set for data resolution? That is, to within at least what tolerance should the difference between two consecutive or nearly consecutive vector-component data be measured? For your information, the current resolution standard for Intermagnet 1-minute data is 0.1 nT. It seems reasonable to expect that 1-second data should meet a somewhat higher resolution standard.
